JACK Quartet

The award-winning JACK Quartet is one of the most exciting ensembles performing in classical music today. Founded at the Eastman School of Music and drawing on the letters of their first names (John, Ari, Chris, and Kevin), JACK focuses almost solely on new and recent works. It is widely acclaimed for its astounding technique, fearless performances, fresh and dynamic programming, and the ability to bring clarity and musicality to the most complex of works. With performances at concert halls and educational institutions around the world, it brings equal care to works by established, emerging and student composers alike.

JACK Quartet’s programming covers the entire spectrum of contemporary music, and balances premières, recent new works and seminal pieces of the modern canon such as Cage, Ligeti, Nancarrow and Xenakis. Helmut Lachenmann was an early champion of JACK, and he invited it to the Lucerne Festival Academy in 2005. There the Quartet worked with Pierre Boulez and the Ensemble intercontemporain, leading to studies with the Kronos Quartet and Arditti Quartet, whom the ensemble count as mentors.

JACK Quartet
  • Christopher Otto - violin
  • Ari Streisfeld - violin
  • John Pickford Richards - viola
  • Kevin McFarland - cello
